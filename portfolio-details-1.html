Portfolio Details

  • Project Date: September 23rd, 2023 - December 02nd, 2023
  • Category: Data Science
  • Project Presentation: SBA Loan Approval

Summary

The Small Business Administration (SBA) utilized a Kaggle dataset, divided into two parts for model development and business simulation. The goal was to predict whether a small business loan recipient would successfully repay or default. The analysis revealed that 32.3% of businesses failed to repay, resulting in a significant financial loss of $5.48 billion for the SBA.

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) uncovered dataset characteristics, identified anomalies, and highlighted class imbalances in the target variable (MIS_Status). Preprocessing involved handling missing values, invalid data, outliers, and feature extraction.

Feature selection was conducted based on statistical tests and correlation analysis. The selected features included both categorical and numerical variables, providing a comprehensive set for predictive modeling.

The chosen model, Decision Tree, underwent hyperparameter tuning to enhance its performance. Evaluation metrics focused on precision and accuracy, emphasizing the importance of reducing false positives due to the business context.

Business simulation demonstrated the model's effectiveness, achieving a precision of 94% and accuracy of 81%. The simulation emphasized reducing false positives and total charged-off amounts as key business metrics.

Post-model implementation, the default percentage decreased from 11.17% to 5.58%, and the total charged-off amount decreased from $3.38 billion to $1.8 billion. The model successfully achieved the business goals of reducing default rates and minimizing financial losses.

Feature importance analysis highlighted critical contributors to the model's predictions. Insights from feature importance led to business recommendations, such as developing long-term loan services, focusing on low default rate industries, and enhancing risk management in high-risk sectors.

In conclusion, the predictive model effectively addressed the SBA's goals, showcasing its potential to enhance decision-making processes in small business loan approval. The combination of robust EDA, preprocessing, feature selection, and model evaluation contributed to the successful implementation of the predictive analytics solution.
